# Mental Health Analysis & Action Plan (July 2026)

Based on recent daily logs and established psychological and medical profiles, this document outlines the root causes of the current anxiety and depressive symptoms and provides actionable strategies to mitigate them.

## 🔍 Root Cause Analysis

1. **Primary Psychological Stressor: The Upcoming Teaching Job**
   - **Trigger:** You have a profound fear of unpreparedness in new situations, combined with hypersensitivity and a tendency to overthink. The pressure of the teaching job starting in September 2026 is acting as a massive subconscious weight, leading to severe morning anxiety and nighttime rumination.
   - **Manifestation:** Anxious thoughts at night about speaking in front of a class, directly contributing to severe insomnia.

2. **Physiological Aggravators**
   - **Caffeine on an Empty Stomach:** Daily logs show consumption of large coffees and Club Mate while skipping breakfast ("couldn't eat solid food"). Caffeine drastically spikes cortisol, which is naturally highest in the morning, directly fueling morning anxiety and panic-like physical symptoms (chest pressure, heart rate).
   - **Gastrointestinal Distress:** The combination of intense anxiety, taking an SSRI (Escitalopram) on an empty stomach, and high caffeine intake is the likely cause of the recent bouts of diarrhea and stomach distress.
   - **Circadian Rhythm Disruption:** Staying awake until 5 AM and waking up at noon creates a cycle of exhaustion. Low energy reduces your ability to employ healthy coping mechanisms (like flow arts).

3. **Behavioral Patterns**
   - **Isolation / Lack of Vulnerability:** Recent introspective experiences highlighted a need to open up. You tend to be a people-pleaser and fear others thinking badly of you, which may prevent you from sharing your fears about the teaching job with your support network.

## 🛠️ Actionable Strategies ("How to get rid of it")

### 1. Structured Preparation (Tackling the Root Fear)

Since unpreparedness is your primary trigger, you need to regain a sense of control over the teaching job.

- **Action:** Break the preparation down into micro-tasks. Do not try to plan the whole semester at once. Schedule just 30 minutes a day to outline the first week's material.
- **Exposure:** Practice speaking out loud in an empty room or in front of your family to desensitize the fear of public speaking.

### 2. Modify the Morning Routine (Stabilizing the Body)

You must stop fueling the physical symptoms of anxiety.

- **Rule 1: No Caffeine Before Food.** You must eat something solid (even just a banana, a small piece of bread, or a portion of your Huel shake) *before* drinking coffee or Club Mate.
- **Rule 2: Hydrate First.** Drink a large glass of water immediately upon waking.
- **Rule 3: Medication Timing.** Take your Escitalopram with your first meal to reduce stomach irritation.

### 3. Regulate the Nervous System (Releasing Adrenaline)

Your body is in a "fight or flight" state in the morning.

- **Action:** Since you lack the energy for full workouts, commit to just **5-10 minutes of flow arts (poi/staff)** or a brisk walk immediately after your morning anxiety peaks. This helps burn off the excess adrenaline.
- **Breathing:** When waking up with fear, use the breathing techniques you know are effective. Treat it as an active requirement, not an optional tool.

### 4. Leverage Community Support (Breaking Isolation)

You prefer talking to people who know you intimately and can challenge your bad behaviors.

- **Action:** Reach out to a close friend or community member this week. Explicitly state: "I am having severe anxiety about my upcoming teaching job and I need to talk about it." Let them help you reality-check your overthinking.

### 5. Sleep Hygiene Reset

- **Action:** If you experience insomnia, do not watch TV. Read a physical book or listen to an audio-only podcast. Force a consistent wake-up time (e.g., 9:00 AM) even if you slept poorly, to force your circadian rhythm back into alignment.
