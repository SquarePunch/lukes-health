# Inherited Knowledge

This document stores distilled, long-term lessons and rules that have proven effective over time.

## Physical Health

- **Shoulder Pain (Ulnaris Nerve):** Extreme shoulder pain due to a clenched ulnaris nerve was successfully resolved with massage therapy. If this pain reoccurs (as it seems to annually), prioritize scheduling massage therapy immediately.
- **Substance Cessation:** Stopping Kratom/Cannabis abruptly after prolonged use has historically triggered rapid mood drops and depressive episodes within weeks. Caution and possibly tapering/medical support should be considered for any future cessation attempts.
- **Stress & Employment:** Taking on high-pressure, time-constrained goals (like rushing self-employment to avoid a job) heavily triggers anxiety, "headless chicken" behavior, and depressive episodes. Stability is key for mental baseline.
- **Substance Interactions (Kratom/Weed + SSRIs):** Mixing Kratom or Cannabis with Escitalopram (Lexapro/SSRI) significantly increases the risk of Serotonin Syndrome, which can be dangerous. It can also cause unexpected metabolic interactions or increase baseline anxiety (especially THC). Strict rule: Do not mix these.
- **Managing PAWS (Post-Acute Withdrawal Syndrome):** After acute withdrawal from Kratom/Weed, psychological symptoms (depression, anxiety, low motivation, cravings) can persist for weeks or months. Recognize these as PAWS rather than a permanent baseline shift.
- **Restoring Balance:** During and after withdrawal, establish strict routines to naturally restore dopamine and serotonin levels. This includes daily exercise, consistent sleep schedules, staying hydrated, and avoiding other substances that spike neurotransmitters artificially.
