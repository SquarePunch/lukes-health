# Substance Reference Guide

This document catalogs researched data on frequently used substances, including their mechanisms of action, long-term effects, withdrawal timelines, and critical interactions with prescribed medications like Escitalopram (SSRI).

## Kratom (Mitragyna speciosa)

### Mechanism of Action

- Low doses (1-5g): Acts primarily as a stimulant by binding to alpha-2 adrenergic receptors.
- High doses (>5g): Acts as a partial agonist at mu-opioid receptors, producing sedative, analgesic, and euphoric effects.

### Short-Term Effects

- Increased energy, alertness, elevated mood, and talkativeness (low doses).
- Pain relief, sedation, euphoria (high doses).
- Side effects can include nausea, constipation, and sweating.

### Long-Term Effects

- Development of tolerance, physical and psychological dependence, and addiction.
- Potential liver toxicity.
- Cognitive impairment and persistent mood swings.

### Withdrawal Timeline

- **Acute Phase (Days 1-7):** Symptoms begin within 6-12 hours of the last dose, peaking around days 2-4. Symptoms include intense muscle/bone aches, nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, severe insomnia, restless legs, sweating, severe anxiety, and cravings. Physical symptoms mostly resolve by day 7.
- **Post-Acute Withdrawal Syndrome (PAWS) (Weeks to Months):** Lingering psychological symptoms such as depression, anxiety, anhedonia (inability to feel pleasure), fatigue, low motivation, and sporadic cravings.

## Cannabis (Weed)

### Mechanism of Action

- Interacts directly with the endocannabinoid system (ECS), primarily through THC binding to CB1 receptors in the brain, altering neurotransmitter release (including dopamine and serotonin).

### Short-Term Effects

- Euphoria, relaxation, altered perception of time, increased appetite.
- Side effects can include paranoia, severe anxiety (especially with high THC strains or edibles), impaired memory, and dry mouth.

### Long-Term Effects

- Downregulation of endocannabinoid receptors, leading to tolerance and dependence.
- Regular use can worsen underlying depression and anxiety over time, rather than curing it.
- Cognitive impairments, specifically affecting short-term memory and attention span.

### Withdrawal Timeline

- **Acute Phase (Days 1-14):** Onset typically 24-48 hours after cessation. Symptoms peak around days 2-6 and include anxiety, irritability, anger, vivid/disturbing dreams, depressed mood, and loss of appetite. Less common physical symptoms include chills, sweating, headaches, and stomach pain.
- **PAWS (Weeks to Months):** Persistent sleep disturbances, lingering anxiety or depressed mood, and cravings.

## Escitalopram (Lexapro)

### Mechanism of Action

- A Selective Serotonin Reuptake Inhibitor (SSRI). It works by blocking the reabsorption (reuptake) of serotonin into neurons, increasing the levels of available serotonin in the synaptic space, which helps improve mood and anxiety.

### Critical Interactions

#### Escitalopram + Kratom

- **Severe Risk of Serotonin Syndrome:** Both kratom and SSRIs can increase serotonin levels. Taking them together can lead to an overload of serotonin in the brain. Symptoms of serotonin syndrome include confusion, agitation, rapid heart rate, high blood pressure, dilated pupils, loss of muscle coordination, muscle rigidity, heavy sweating, and in severe cases, seizures and death.
- **CNS Depression:** Combining these can lead to increased sedation and respiratory depression.

#### Escitalopram + Cannabis (Weed)

- **Increased Risk of Serotonin Syndrome:** Marijuana also interacts with neurotransmitters and can increase serotonin levels. Mixing it with antidepressants can cause a dangerous serotonin overload.
- **Metabolic Interactions:** Cannabis use can induce or inhibit liver enzymes responsible for breaking down Lexapro. This means cannabis can either make Lexapro less effective (by breaking it down too quickly) or lead to toxic levels of Lexapro in the blood (by inhibiting its breakdown).
- **Worsened Psychiatric Symptoms:** While some use weed to self-medicate, high THC content often exacerbates the very anxiety and depression that Escitalopram is meant to treat.
