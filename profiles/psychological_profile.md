# Psychological Profile

## Baseline & Personality

- **Intelligence:** IQ 130
- **Traits:** Hypersensitive, prone to overthinking.
- **Values & Motivations:** Highly values time to do what they love (creating art, software, writing). Values spending time with friends (partying) and talking to/being there for family. Losing track of time in a positive way usually happens when working on art, software, or writing.
- **Social Interaction:** Finds it hard to talk to people until knowing them well and knowing exactly what to say. Being with a lot of new people without any acquaintances causes some discomfort but isn't a primary anxiety trigger. Despite earlier fears, public speaking (like at conferences) has become easier with experience, provided there is thorough preparation.
- **Behavioral Tendencies:** Takes responsibility whenever seen. Strong need for harmony, tends to be a people-pleaser rather than prioritizing own needs. This stems from a fear of others thinking badly of them and a strong desire to be admired and valued.
- **Career:** Software engineer since 2008. Self-employed 2020-2023 (founded 1-person company). Took a 3-day (25h)/week job in 2024. Quit early 2026 due to mental health. Starts teaching job in September 2026.
- **Family:** Wife and two daughters (ages 13, 16). Needs occasional alone time.

## Triggers & Stressors

- Pressure to succeed or earn money in a limited timeframe (led to "headless chicken" behavior and a depressive episode in 2026).
- Job interviews (triggers anxiety, previously coped using Kratom). Unpreparedness in new situations.
- Stopping substance use abruptly has historically triggered rapid mood drops within weeks.

- **Mid-2025:** Depressive episode with panic attacks following 5 weeks of sobriety from Kratom/Cannabis. Symptoms: Waking up in fear in the morning, mood improving over the day, feeling normal by the evening.
- **Early 2026:** Another depressive episode leading to quitting the 25h/week job (occurred despite being on SSRI).
- **Mid-2026:** Relapsed into depression due to self-employment stress after stopping SSRI in April 2026.
- **Current State (July 2026):** Morning anxiety returned. Feeling exhausted but not entirely depressed. Mood typically improves by noon.
- **Physical Symptoms:** Panic attacks or anxiety are preceded by increased heart rate and chest pressure. Depressive drops and anxiety both come with a lack of energy and hunger.
